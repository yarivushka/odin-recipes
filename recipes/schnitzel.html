What is Schnitzel?

A Schnitzel is a thin slice of meat, breaded before frying. Breaded schnitzel is popular in many countries and is made using veal, pork, chicken, mutton, beef, or turkey. Schnitzel is very similar to the dish escalope in France, tonkatsu in Japan, cotoletta in Italy, kotlet schabowy in Poland, milanesa in Argentina, chuleta valluna in Colombia, and chicken-fried steak and pork tenderloin of the United States.

In short, it is an internationally common food that has different names in different cultures.

Ingredients
  • Your preferred kind of meat (commonly it is made out of veal, pork, chicken, mutton, beef, or turkey), thinned!
  • Beaten eggs
  • Bread crumbs
  • Mustard, Salt and Pepper
  • Oil
Steps
  1. Put bread crumbs in a deep dish, large enough for the meat chuncks to fit in.
  2. Beat eggs in a deep dish (again, large enough for the meat chuncks to fit in) with mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dip meat in eggs, then coat it with bread crumbs, then transfer to a clean dish.
  4. Warm the oil so it will bublle if you put a bit of bread crumbs in it.
  5. Fry breaded meat from one side till it browns, then turn over to do the same with other side.
  6. Take out causiously to a clean dish.
  7. Serve hot with a slice of lemon and french fries or baked potatoes.
